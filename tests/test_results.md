• **2025年秋季正式停止提供AP课程**：韦伯学校在2025年秋季正式停止提供所有高级先修课程(Advanced Placement courses)

### ✅ life-02: What is dorm life like at Webb?

**Coverage**: 100% | **Time**: 26.2s | **Language**: en

**Answer:**

Based on Webb Schools' official documents, here's what dorm life is like:

## Living Environment & Community
Dorm life is **central to the Webb experience**, serving as a hub of friendship, activity, and community. Each student's room is their personal space for sleeping and studying. Webb has unique California architecture - from the historic arches of Alamo (the oldest dorm) to Jameson's balconies with stunning San Gabriel Mountain views.

**Nearly all of Webb's dorms are singles**, giving students their own private space. All dorms have live-in **Dorm Heads** who form strong bonds with residents and host activities like cooking, baking, winter fire pits, and marshmallow roasting.

## Daily Schedule & Structure
**Weekday Evening Schedule:**
- **7:30 PM**: All boarding students check in at their dorm
- Students can attend academic labs for homework help or study in the library
- **9:00 PM**: Final face-to-face check-in with on-duty faculty
- After 9 PM check-in, students must remain in their dorm area for the evening

**Lights Out Times:**
- **Freshmen**: 10:30 PM (lights out)
- **Sophomores**: 11:00 PM (lights out) 
- **Juniors**: Must be in rooms by 11 PM, lights out by 12 AM
- **Seniors**: Must be in own rooms by 11 PM, lights out by 12 AM

## Room Care & Decoration
Students are **largely permitted to decorate their dorms** to create comfortable, individualized spaces, with guidelines:

**Room Inspection Requirements** (formal checks twice weekly, plus monthly thorough checks):
1. Make their bed
2. Keep floor swept/vacuumed
3. Keep desk neat, clean, and organized
4. Stow all laundry in hamper or bag

**Decoration Rules:**
- Use 3M wall hooks with Command Adhesive or removable putty only
- No nails or tape (except painter's tape)
- No liquor bottles as decoration
- No ceiling decorations without dorm head permission
- Beds must remain on frames
- Built-in furniture cannot be moved

## What's Allowed vs. Prohibited in Rooms

**Permitted:**
- Table flatware only
- Small refrigerators (max 2' x 2' x 4')
- Photography gear and computers (not covered by school insurance)

**Not Permitted:**
- Air conditioners or large appliances
- Candles with open flames
- Cooking appliances (microwaves, toasters, rice cookers, hot pots, etc.)
- Space heaters, halogen lamps, electric blankets

### ✅ edge-06: What is the Alf Museum?

**Coverage**: 100% | **Time**: 11.1s | **Language**: en

**Answer:**

The **Alf Museum** (formally the Raymond M. Alf Museum of Paleontology) is a paleontology museum located on The Webb Schools campus that offers unique educational opportunities for students.

## Key Facts About the Alf Museum:

• **Only nationally accredited museum in the USA on a high school campus** - achieved accreditation from the American Association of Museums in 1998 (an honor less than 5% of US museums can claim)

• **World's only paleontology museum on a high school campus**

• **Unique student research program** - Webb students work hand-in-hand with professional scientists in the field and laboratory, studying fossils they find on collecting trips and publishing research results in collaboration with museum staff

## Student Involvement:
• Students participate in **Peccary Trips** (fossil collecting expeditions named after Webb's fossil peccary discovery)
• Students work alongside professional fossil preparators in the museum's fossil prep lab
• **Over 50 Webb students** have contributed to and published scientific papers in paleontology
• Students co-author research articles with museum staff that are published in regional and international publications

## History:
The museum was founded by **Raymond Alf**, a teacher who came to Webb in 1929. In 1936, Alf and student Bill Webb '39 discovered a 15-million-year-old fossil peccary skull in the Mojave Desert, which was identified as a new species. This discovery launched Alf's lifelong quest to study "the documents of life" through fossils. The current museum building was dedicated in 1968.

The Alf Museum exemplifies Webb's **"learning by doing" philosophy**, offering students an unparalleled opportunity to engage in authentic scientific research at the high school level.
