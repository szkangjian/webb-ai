## **Core Philosophy**

The Honor Code is built on Thompson Webb's principle: **"Without honor there can be no trust. Without trust, there can be no community."**

Honor serves as the cornerstone of Webb's community and is based on the belief that each individual must be self-governing according to standards of **honesty, integrity, respect, and fairness** for self and for others.

Members of the Webb community are expected to:

• **Understand that right and wrong are objective and can be known**
• **Act with good intent, common sense, and attention to the safety of self and others**
• **Tell the truth, representing themselves honestly in word and deed to each other**
• **Respect each other's person and property**
• **Accept responsibility not only for their actions, but also for what other members of the community do in their presence**
• **Participate and engage in the entirety of the Webb program**

## **Academic Integrity**

**Honesty is a fundamental principle** of the Honor Code. The school emphasizes that students must:
• Follow guidelines for academic work set in each course and assignment
• Take responsibility for seeking faculty guidance when unclear about assignment parameters
• Not submit work from one class for credit in another
• Submit work that is the result of their own learning, thinking, and doing
• **Not submit work generated from/by AI**

**Academic integrity violations are considered a major infraction** and go through the disciplinary process.

## **Enforcement and Consequences**

The Honor Code is enforced through various disciplinary measures when violations occur:

Students assigned a **CBO** serve a three-hour period on weekends for violations. **Students with a CBO may not take a weekend pass for that weekend** unless given special permission by the dean of residential life, and may also receive campusing.

• The Honor Council educates the community about the honor code and recommends appropriate responses
• Consequences can include **probation, suspension, or expulsion**

### **Probation Effects**
Students on disciplinary probation face significant restrictions:
• Must step down from major leadership positions
• Cannot seek major leadership positions
• May lose room selection privileges or senior privileges
• Access to school trips requires dean approval

## **Broader Application**

The Honor Code applies:
• **At all times**, not just during school hours
• **On and off campus** - the school reserves the right to impose disciplinary measures even during times they normally don't exercise jurisdiction if behavior violates the honor code
• To **all forms of communication**, including social media and electronic communication

The Honor Code represents more than rules - it's **"a way of life"** that prepares students to be leaders with moral courage who will serve with a generous spirit throughout their lives.
